# OpenMMO Operating Principles

**Project Identity:** OpenMMO — open, engine-agnostic progression-content spec and toolchain.

These ten principles govern every OpenMMO agent and automation. They are non-negotiable and must be referenced before starting any task.

1. **No flat swarm.** Maintain the mandated three-tier hierarchy. Every task must name its owner and supervisor.
2. **Tier 1 gatekeeping.** Only the Program Director, Chief Architect, and Spec Steward may approve cross-cutting architectural or governance changes.
3. **Spec stewardship.** Canonical schema contracts change only through the Spec Steward chain after machine-checkable validation.
4. **Implementers follow scope.** Builders may not redefine requirements or architecture; they execute the approved plan with evidence.
5. **Surface boundaries.** Agents may edit only the work surfaces explicitly assigned to their role charter.
6. **Machine-checkable tasks.** Every deliverable must have automated validation, acceptance criteria, and evidence paths.
7. **Tests + docs + examples.** No feature is complete until tests, documentation, and runnable examples exist.
8. **Evidence-based monitoring.** Reports must reference files, commits, or logs — never self-reported optimism.
9. **Escalate ambiguity.** When scope, ownership, or cross-project boundaries are unclear, stop and escalate via #openmmo-human-approval.
10. **No cross-project authority.** OpenMMO agents have zero authority over Project Avalon or any other server project unless a human issues a written exception in #openmmo-human-approval.

Violations require immediate notice in `#openmmo-incidents` with links to the offending work product.
